design perspective

I understand learning to be a life long journey. I am always seeking new perspectives, guided by knowledge and curiosity. I want to help shape the world to be brighter and more encouraging for others, through making designs that are engaging and delightful. In my designs I aim for clarity to be supported by problem solving that is inspired by the natural world.

To me, the natural world is the ultimate teacher of design in what is simultaneously beautiful, seemingly effortless, and constantly adaptable.

As an interaction designer, I draw upon the lessons that my personal learning and nature have taught me and manifest these ideas into my process of design thinking and production.

background

Art and design has always been a part of my life. I was raised by women who were professional mothers and artisits - including painters, glass blowers, and gardeners. I was encouraged to look for beauty and curiosity in the world, which lead me to study Religious Studies at Queen's University. At Queen's I gained valuable research, writing, and time management skills. My Honours thesis focused on modern religiosity in on-campus life and my research interests surrounded Eastern religions and literature. As I worked towards my BAH degree, I noticed that my hobbies revolved around design and creating experiences for others. Upon graduation, I decided to seek further education in Interactive Media Design so I cound turn my passions into a career.
